Frequently Asked Questions

Fast fashion refers to the rapid production and consumption of inexpensive clothing items that follow the latest trends. The fast fashion industry contributes to environmental problems through excessive resource consumption, water pollution, greenhouse gas emissions, and increased textile waste due to its short product life cycle.

Fast fashion requires vast amounts of water for dyeing, printing, and finishing textiles. The chemical-laden wastewater from textile factories is often discharged into water bodies, leading to water pollution and harming aquatic ecosystems.

Fast fashion's business model encourages consumers to buy more, leading to a higher turnover of clothes. As a result, discarded garments and textiles end up in landfills, adding to the growing problem of textile waste.

Textile waste in landfills can release methane, a potent greenhouse gas, as the garments decompose. Additionally, the dyes and chemicals from the fabrics can leach into the soil and contaminate nearby water sources.

The fashion industry can invest in eco-friendly materials, improve supply chain transparency, implement water and energy-saving technologies, adopt circular economy principles, and encourage designs that promote clothing longevity and versatility.

Buy less, choose well, and make it last! Support ethical brands, shop secondhand, and invest in quality pieces.

Donate, swap, or upcycle your used clothing, and always consider repurposing items before discarding them.

Textile recycling reclaims used fabrics, repurposing them into new products or breaking them down to create new textiles.
